What is Solar PV?

Solar Photovoltaic (PV) technology captures the sun’s energy and converts it into electricity. Unlike solar thermal which uses heat, PV uses semi-conductors—typically silicon—to create a flow of electrons. This direct current (DC) is then converted by an inverter into alternating current (AC), which powers your home, office, or industrial machinery.

Solar PV Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to help you make an informed transition to renewable energy.

How long do Solar PV panels last?

Most high-quality PV panels come with a 25-year performance warranty. While their efficiency slightly degrades over time (about 0.5% per year), they can continue to generate electricity for 30 years or more.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days or at night?

Solar panels still generate power during cloudy days, though at a reduced rate. They do not generate power at night. This is why we recommend battery storage systems to provide energy after sunset.

Will solar help during Load Shedding?

Yes, provided you have a hybrid or off-grid system with an inverter and battery storage. Standard grid-tied systems without batteries automatically shut down during power outages for safety reasons (Anti-islanding).

How much roof space do I need?

A typical residential system requires between 20 to 50 square meters of roof space, depending on your energy needs. We conduct a detailed site audit to optimize panel placement for maximum irradiance.
